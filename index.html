This website made for the educational purpose to realize the scale of our Universe compared to 1 pixel on your screen.

UNIVERSE

Let's imagine our known Universe is this blue circle on your screen and Laniakea Supercluster which is 520,000,000 light years long including Milky Way and other 100,000 galaxies is just 1 pixel at the center. Then our Universe's diameter is 179 pixels or 93,000,000,000 light years.

The biggest known star

Rigel is a seventh brightest star in the night sky. It is an enormous blue-white supergiant, the biggest one of its multiple star system. Rigel-B is a second brightest star of this system actually reveals a triple star system itself, but we can't see them with a naked eye.

The largest known star is UY Scuti with diameter 2,400,000,000 km while Arcturus with diameter 36,000,000 km is 1 pixel starting point. UY Scuti is a red supergiant located 9,500 light years from the Sun closer to Milky Way core and it has 340,000 times sun's luminosity.

You can realize the size of UY Scuti in a comparison with Neptune's orbit.

SOLAR SYSTEM DISTANCES SCALE

Now let's imagine that our Sun diameter (which is 1,391,400 km) just 1 pixel on your screen. So then we can scale distances to planets regarding that one pixel. Unfortunately, we can't represent diameters of planets properly as in this case it's much smaller than 1 pixel so we represent planets as same 1 pixel. Here we just want to realize how far planets apart from the Sun and from each other. Inaccuracy comes with distance rounding to pixel and limitations of an object's sizes. So inaccuracy in this scale is about 1 pixel.
